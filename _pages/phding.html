I am in the third year (out of three I hope!) of my PhD, and I am broadly investigating how donating blood impacts donors' health. I am fortunate to be funded by the National Institute for Health and Care Research (NIHR), supervised by Dr Lois Kim, and advised by Dr Stephen Kaptoge. You can read more about our research group here!

What I'm up to this week [07/02/2026]

The fun continues ... I have been complexifying (sp.?) my analyses of dietary behaviours in blood donors because I have realised that I have to deal with time-varying confounding ... which has given me the chance to learn about causal inference methods that I have been fangirling over for some time. I've also done an overhaul of my tables and figures for a new manuscript describing the impacts of donation on long-term health, made some headway with even more job applications, and basked in the joy of learning with and from masters' students during a TA session. See you next week!

Undergraduate courses

I currently supervise first-year medical students in Foundations of Evidence-Based Practice (4 weeks). In Cambridge, supervisions are small-group teaching sessions where learners discuss lecture content and its real-world applications with someone already working in their field of study.

Postgraduate courses

I have served as a teaching assistant (~12 hours) and exam grader (~12 hours) for statistics and epidemiology modules within the MPhil in Population Health Sciences at the University of Cambridge. I have also assisted with the design of assessment materials for an advanced biostatistics module within the MPhil.
